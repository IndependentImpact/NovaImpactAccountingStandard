1. Purpose of the Activity

Validation
•	Does the stated purpose clearly define the intended positive impact and its link to the target environment? (Relevance)
•	Is the justification for the activity’s purpose complete and supported by evidence or baseline data? (Completeness)
•	Is the purpose formulated in a way that aligns with comparable activities or prior commitments? (Consistency)
•	Does the description of the purpose clearly disclose any assumptions, intended beneficiaries, and expected co-benefits? (Transparency)

Verification
•	Is the implemented activity consistent with its original stated purpose? (Consistency)
•	Were any material changes to the activity’s purpose or objectives disclosed and justified? (Transparency)
•	Does the observed outcome correspond to the intended type of impact? (Relevance)

2. Target Environment

Validation
•	Has the target environment been accurately characterized (ecological, social, or economic baseline)? (Accuracy)
•	Are the boundaries of the target environment appropriately defined and justified? (Completeness)
•	Is the environment chosen relevant to the stated purpose and intervention measures? (Relevance)
•	Are there foreseeable risks of reversal or degradation of benefits in this environment? (Permanence)

Verification
•	Does the monitored environment correspond to the originally defined boundaries? (Completeness)
•	Are the environmental data used for assessment accurate, verifiable, and properly sourced? (Accuracy)
•	Have measures been taken to maintain permanence of impacts (e.g. maintenance, stewardship, legal safeguards)? (Permanence)

3. Intervention Measures

Validation
•	Are the proposed intervention measures demonstrably relevant to achieving the stated purpose? (Relevance)
•	Are the technical and procedural descriptions of measures internally consistent and replicable? (Consistency)
•	Do the projected outcomes rely on scientifically sound or evidence-based assumptions? (Accuracy)
•	Are assumptions regarding expected impacts conservative and not likely to overstate benefits? (Conservativeness)
•	Have permanence risks been identified and mitigation strategies proposed? (Permanence)

Verification
•	Were the intervention measures implemented as planned, without unapproved deviations? (Consistency)
•	Is there evidence supporting that the interventions performed as described? (Accuracy)
•	Are any observed deviations transparently justified and conservatively treated in impact accounting? (Transparency + Conservativeness)
•	Are long-term maintenance or protection mechanisms functioning as intended? (Permanence)

4. Monitoring Sample

Validation
•	Is the proposed sampling design relevant to the selected indicators and the target environment? (Relevance)
•	Are the sampling methods statistically sound and expected to yield accurate data? (Accuracy)
•	Are sample sizes and frequencies consistent with recognized standards or prior studies? (Consistency)
•	Have any limitations in sampling representativeness been disclosed? (Transparency)
•	Are conservative margins or confidence levels applied to avoid overestimating positive impacts? (Conservativeness)

Verification
•	Was the sampling carried out according to the approved design? (Consistency)
•	Are the sample data traceable and reproducible? (Transparency + Accuracy)
•	Have errors, biases, or anomalies been transparently disclosed and conservatively treated? (Conservativeness)

5. Impact Indicators

Validation
•	Are the selected indicators relevant and directly linked to the intended impacts? (Relevance)
•	Do they cover all material dimensions of the intended impact (ecological, social, etc.)? (Completeness)
•	Are definitions, units, and baselines clearly specified? (Accuracy)
•	Are indicators consistent with those used in comparable projects or sectoral methodologies? (Consistency)
•	Are the data sources and calculation methods fully disclosed? (Transparency)

Verification
•	Are the indicators applied during monitoring identical to those validated? (Consistency)
•	Is indicator data accurately measured and reported? (Accuracy)
•	Were any indicator substitutions or adjustments transparently documented and justified? (Transparency)

6. Monitoring Methods

Validation
•	Are the proposed monitoring methods relevant to the selected indicators and the target environment? (Relevance)
•	Are the methods consistent with recognized scientific or industry standards? (Consistency)
•	Are the methodologies, instruments, and data collection protocols fully described and replicable? (Transparency)
•	Do the methods provide sufficient precision to ensure accurate impact measurement? (Accuracy)
•	Have conservative assumptions been applied to reduce the risk of overestimating positive impacts? (Conservativeness)

Verification
•	Were the monitoring methods applied as described in the validation documentation? (Consistency)
•	Are calibration, maintenance, and procedural records available to confirm data integrity? (Accuracy + Transparency)
•	Were any deviations from the approved methods transparently justified and conservatively treated? (Transparency + Conservativeness)

7. Assessment of Risks Associated with the Activity

Validation
•	Have all relevant environmental, social, and operational risks been identified and characterized? (Completeness)
•	Are the identified risks relevant to the activity’s context and environment? (Relevance)
•	Are risk assessment criteria and mitigation plans applied consistently with sectoral or regulatory norms? (Consistency)
•	Are the sources, assumptions, and models used for risk assessment transparently disclosed? (Transparency)
•	Does the risk analysis include potential reversals or loss of impact permanence? (Permanence)

Verification
•	Were identified risks effectively monitored and managed during implementation? (Consistency)
•	Were any new or unanticipated risks documented and disclosed? (Transparency)
•	Did any risk events occur that threaten the permanence or credibility of impacts? (Permanence)

8. Stakeholder Consultation Process(es)

Validation
•	Were all relevant stakeholder groups (including vulnerable or affected parties) identified and invited to participate? (Completeness)
•	Were consultation methods appropriate to the social and cultural context of the activity? (Relevance)
•	Were consultations conducted consistently and documented according to good practice standards? (Consistency)
•	Are consultation records, meeting notes, and feedback mechanisms transparently documented? (Transparency)
•	Were stakeholder concerns incorporated conservatively to avoid overstating consensus or support? (Conservativeness)

Verification
•	Did the consultation process occur as validated (scope, timing, participation)? (Consistency)
•	Are records of actual stakeholder engagement available and verifiable? (Transparency)
•	Were post-implementation feedback or grievance mechanisms maintained and effective? (Completeness + Transparency)

9. Responses to Stakeholder Issues

Validation
•	Have all significant stakeholder concerns been reviewed and addressed in the design phase? (Completeness + Relevance)
•	Are the planned responses appropriate, evidence-based, and proportional to the issues raised? (Accuracy + Relevance)
•	Are commitments to modify or mitigate aspects of the activity transparently documented? (Transparency)

Verification
•	Were all stakeholder concerns actually addressed as committed during validation? (Consistency)
•	Are records of responses, changes, or mitigations traceable and verifiable? (Transparency)
•	Were unresolved or residual issues conservatively reported rather than minimized? (Conservativeness)

10. Computations and Calculations

Validation
•	Are all relevant variables, parameters, and equations defined and justified? (Completeness + Transparency)
•	Are the computational methods consistent with established accounting or modelling standards? (Consistency)
•	Are uncertainty factors and error margins appropriately considered? (Accuracy + Conservativeness)
•	Is the documentation of formulas and data sources sufficient for full replication? (Transparency)

Verification
•	Are calculations performed as validated and reproducible with the reported data? (Consistency + Transparency)
•	Are results free from arithmetical or methodological errors? (Accuracy)
•	Are conservative assumptions applied when uncertainties arise? (Conservativeness)

11. QA & QC Measures for Intervention Measures

Validation
•	Are QA/QC procedures for intervention design and implementation clearly defined? (Transparency + Consistency)
•	Are roles, responsibilities, and verification steps specified for implementation oversight? (Consistency)
•	Are quality thresholds or acceptance criteria evidence-based and appropriate? (Accuracy)
•	Do QA/QC plans include provisions for conservative error treatment or corrective action? (Conservativeness)

Verification
•	Were QA/QC procedures implemented as planned, and are records available? (Consistency + Transparency)
•	Did QA/QC checks effectively detect and correct non-conformities? (Accuracy)
•	Were deviations handled conservatively to avoid overstating impact performance? (Conservativeness)

12. QA & QC Measures for Implementation Data

Validation
•	Are data QA/QC protocols clearly described (validation, cross-checks, audits)? (Transparency + Consistency)
•	Are data collection instruments and verification methods designed to ensure accuracy? (Accuracy)
•	Are conservative procedures planned for uncertain or missing data? (Conservativeness)

Verification
•	Are QA/QC protocols demonstrably applied to all implementation data? (Consistency)
•	Are all raw data, metadata, and audit trails available for review? (Transparency)
•	Have data discrepancies been resolved conservatively and documented? (Conservativeness)

13. QA & QC Measures for Impact Monitoring Data

Validation
•	Are procedures defined to ensure the accuracy and repeatability of impact measurements? (Accuracy + Consistency)
•	Are error margins, calibration checks, and review cycles specified? (Accuracy)
•	Are documentation and traceability requirements clear and enforceable? (Transparency)

Verification
•	Were QA/QC checks applied to all impact data sets as validated? (Consistency)
•	Are anomalies, outliers, or data gaps documented and explained? (Transparency)
•	Have uncertainties been handled conservatively to avoid overestimating impact magnitude? (Conservativeness)

14. Additionality of the Impact(s)

Validation
•	Is the baseline scenario clearly defined and relevant to the claimed impact? (Relevance)
•	Are all plausible alternative scenarios considered to demonstrate additionality? (Completeness)
•	Are quantitative or qualitative justifications accurate and evidence-based? (Accuracy)
•	Are conservative assumptions applied to prevent overestimation of additionality? (Conservativeness)

Verification
•	Does observed performance confirm that the impact would not have occurred without the activity? (Relevance + Accuracy)
•	Are deviations from baseline assumptions documented and justified? (Transparency)
•	Is additionality maintained and conservatively interpreted where uncertainties exist? (Conservativeness)

15. Parties and Participants to the Activity

Validation
•	Are all parties and participants clearly identified with their roles and responsibilities? (Completeness)
•	Are governance, decision-making, and oversight structures transparently described? (Transparency)
•	Are institutional and contractual arrangements consistent with the activity’s objectives and regulations? (Consistency)

Verification
•	Were the identified parties and participants involved as planned? (Consistency)
•	Are any changes in participation or control transparently reported? (Transparency)
•	Were roles fulfilled as validated and documented? (Completeness)

16. Legal Aspects (e.g., Land Ownership Rights)

Validation
•	Are all relevant legal rights (e.g., land tenure, resource access) identified and documented? (Completeness)
•	Are legal claims relevant to the activity’s implementation and permanence of impacts? (Relevance + Permanence)
•	Are ownership and consent documents transparently available and verifiable? (Transparency)

Verification
•	Are legal arrangements still valid and respected during implementation? (Consistency + Permanence)
•	Have any disputes, transfers, or changes in rights been disclosed? (Transparency)
•	Do current legal conditions still support the long-term permanence of impacts? (Permanence)

17. Funding Mechanisms and Sources

Validation
•	Are all funding sources, instruments, and flows disclosed? (Completeness + Transparency)
•	Are financial mechanisms consistent with the activity’s goals and timeline? (Consistency)
•	Are provisions in place to ensure long-term funding for impact permanence? (Permanence)

Verification
•	Have funds been disbursed as planned and transparently accounted for? (Transparency + Consistency)
•	Are any deviations or new sources disclosed and justified? (Transparency)
•	Is funding stability sufficient to maintain long-term benefits? (Permanence)

18. Fairness of Compensation

Validation
•	Are all compensated parties identified, and the basis for compensation documented? (Completeness)
•	Are compensation rates accurate and fair in relation to local or sectoral norms? (Accuracy)
•	Are compensation structures transparently disclosed? (Transparency)
•	Are conservative assumptions applied to prevent underestimation of due compensation? (Conservativeness)

Verification
•	Were compensations paid as validated and documented? (Consistency + Transparency)
•	Is there evidence that all relevant parties received fair compensation? (Accuracy + Completeness)
•	Were any discrepancies resolved conservatively in favour of affected parties? (Conservativeness)

19. Expenditure of Funds

Validation
•	Is the proposed budget complete, with all categories and contingencies included? (Completeness)
•	Are budget assumptions and allocations consistent with activity design? (Consistency)
•	Are financial management procedures transparent and verifiable? (Transparency)
•	Are cost estimates accurate and based on realistic data? (Accuracy)

Verification
•	Are expenditures traceable and supported by verifiable documentation? (Transparency + Accuracy)
•	Are expenditures consistent with the validated budget structure? (Consistency)
•	Are any budget revisions disclosed and justified? (Transparency)
