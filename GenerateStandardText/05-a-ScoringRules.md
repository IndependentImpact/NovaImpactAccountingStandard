## Scoring Rules

### Indicator quality dimensions

Controlled vocabulary of quality dimensions used to assess the fitness-for-purpose of impact indicators, covering validity, reliability, and sensitivity.

| Term | Definition | IRI |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Reliability | The degree to which an indicator produces consistent results when measured repeatedly under similar conditions. | https://nova.org.za/novaimpactaccountingstandard/indicator-reliability |
| Sensitivity | The ability of an indicator to detect meaningful differences or changes in the underlying reality, including timeliness (whether the indicator updates fast enough to reflect changes in a useful timeframe). | https://nova.org.za/novaimpactaccountingstandard/indicator-sensitivity |
| Validity | The degree to which an indicator measures what it is intended to measure and correctly reflects the underlying concept, including specificity (uniqueness of the indicator to the state of interest). | https://nova.org.za/novaimpactaccountingstandard/indicator-validity |

### Instrument quality dimensions

Controlled vocabulary of quality dimensions used to assess the fitness-for-purpose of measurement instruments used in impact monitoring, covering accuracy, precision, resolution, sensitivity, range, stability, calibration, selectivity, noise, response time, robustness, reliability, usability, and traceability.

| Term | Definition | IRI |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Accuracy | The closeness of a measurement instrument's readings to the true value of the quantity being measured; requires calibration against a known standard. | https://nova.org.za/novaimpactaccountingstandard/instrument-accuracy |
| Calibration | The process and frequency of adjusting a measurement instrument against a known reference standard to ensure its readings remain accurate. | https://nova.org.za/novaimpactaccountingstandard/instrument-calibration |
| Noise | The amount of random variation present in a measurement instrument's output; a high signal-to-noise ratio is required to reliably distinguish real changes from measurement artefacts. | https://nova.org.za/novaimpactaccountingstandard/instrument-noise |
| Precision | The repeatability of a measurement instrument — the degree to which repeated measurements of the same quantity under similar conditions yield the same result. | https://nova.org.za/novaimpactaccountingstandard/instrument-precision |
| Range | The interval of values over which a measurement instrument can produce reliable measurements. | https://nova.org.za/novaimpactaccountingstandard/instrument-range |
| Reliability | The consistency of a measurement instrument's operational uptime — the degree to which it functions without failure over time. | https://nova.org.za/novaimpactaccountingstandard/instrument-reliability |
| Resolution | The smallest change in the measured quantity that a measurement instrument can detect and report. | https://nova.org.za/novaimpactaccountingstandard/instrument-resolution |
| Response time | The speed at which a measurement instrument reacts to changes in the measured quantity. | https://nova.org.za/novaimpactaccountingstandard/instrument-response-time |
| Robustness | The ability of a measurement instrument to maintain reliable performance under real-world environmental conditions such as temperature, humidity, and vibration. | https://nova.org.za/novaimpactaccountingstandard/instrument-robustness |
| Selectivity | The degree to which a measurement instrument measures only the intended variable without being influenced by other variables (specificity at the instrument level). | https://nova.org.za/novaimpactaccountingstandard/instrument-selectivity |
| Sensitivity | The magnitude of a measurement instrument's output change for a given change in the measured quantity. | https://nova.org.za/novaimpactaccountingstandard/instrument-sensitivity |
| Stability | The ability of a measurement instrument to maintain its performance characteristics over time without drifting out of calibration. | https://nova.org.za/novaimpactaccountingstandard/instrument-stability |
| Traceability | The ability to link a measurement instrument's readings back to recognised national or international measurement standards, supporting scientific credibility and regulatory compliance. | https://nova.org.za/novaimpactaccountingstandard/instrument-traceability |
| Usability | The ease with which a measurement instrument can be correctly operated by its intended users, reducing the risk of operator error. | https://nova.org.za/novaimpactaccountingstandard/instrument-usability |

### Methodology quality dimensions

Controlled vocabulary of quality dimensions used to assess the fitness-for-purpose of impact methodologies, covering conceptual soundness, internal and external validity, reliability, transparency, robustness, sensitivity, completeness, scalability, timeliness, and normative alignment.

| Term | Definition | IRI |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Completeness | The degree to which a methodology's system boundary includes all relevant factors such as leakage, rebound effects, and supply-chain impacts, avoiding the exclusion of material negative consequences. | https://nova.org.za/novaimpactaccountingstandard/methodology-completeness |
| Conceptual soundness | The degree to which a methodology is grounded in a clear and correct model of reality that explains why the chosen indicators relate to the intended outcome. | https://nova.org.za/novaimpactaccountingstandard/methodology-conceptual-soundness |
| External validity | The degree to which a methodology's results generalise across different contexts, populations, or conditions beyond the settings in which it was originally validated. | https://nova.org.za/novaimpactaccountingstandard/methodology-external-validity |
| Internal validity | The degree to which a methodology correctly attributes cause and effect, isolating the impact of the intervention and avoiding confounding variables or spurious correlations. | https://nova.org.za/novaimpactaccountingstandard/methodology-internal-validity |
| Normative alignment | The degree to which a methodology is fit for the purpose it is meant to serve, aligning with the normative goals of the standard or decision context rather than being optimised for reporting alone. | https://nova.org.za/novaimpactaccountingstandard/methodology-normative-alignment |
| Reliability | The degree to which independent practitioners applying the same methodology to the same data obtain similar results, reflecting clear procedures and low dependence on subjective judgment. | https://nova.org.za/novaimpactaccountingstandard/methodology-reliability |
| Robustness | The degree to which a methodology produces consistent and trustworthy results under imperfect real-world data conditions and is resistant to manipulation or gaming. | https://nova.org.za/novaimpactaccountingstandard/methodology-robustness |
| Scalability & practicality | The degree to which a methodology can be applied at scale in a cost-effective manner, making it practically usable beyond pilot conditions. | https://nova.org.za/novaimpactaccountingstandard/methodology-scalability |
| Sensitivity & specificity | The degree to which a methodology as a whole reliably detects genuine effects (sensitivity) while avoiding false-positive impact claims (specificity). | https://nova.org.za/novaimpactaccountingstandard/methodology-sensitivity-specificity |
| Timeliness | The degree to which a methodology produces insights quickly enough to support monitoring and adaptive decision-making. | https://nova.org.za/novaimpactaccountingstandard/methodology-timeliness |
| Transparency | The degree to which a methodology's assumptions, data provenance, and procedural steps are documented and accessible so that independent parties can inspect, understand, and verify the methodology. | https://nova.org.za/novaimpactaccountingstandard/methodology-transparency |

### Review mandate

Controlled vocabulary distinguishing the two types of review mandate under the Nova Impact Accounting Standard: validation (ex-ante design review) and verification (ex-post implementation review).

| Term | Definition | IRI |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Validation | A review mandate that evaluates the design of an activity before implementation (ex ante), assessing the likelihood that the activity will, if implemented according to its design, achieve its intended impacts while complying with the applicable standard. | https://nova.org.za/novaimpactaccountingstandard/validation |
| Verification | A review mandate that evaluates an activity and its impacts during or after implementation and impact monitoring (ex post), assessing what actually occurred during implementation and monitoring. | https://nova.org.za/novaimpactaccountingstandard/verification |

### Scoring model terms

Controlled vocabulary of terms used in the Nova Impact Accounting Standard activity scoring model, including score types, scoring units, expert thresholds, and reputation-weighted adjustments.

| Term | Definition | IRI |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ActSco | Activity Score — a composite score reflecting the quality of an activity's design and implementation as assessed through validation and verification reviews. | https://nova.org.za/novaimpactaccountingstandard/act-sco |
| Expert threshold | The minimum Knowledge & Skills reputation amount in a domain that a standard requires a reviewer to hold in order to be considered an expert for that domain. Reviews submitted below the expert threshold are weighted proportionally. | https://nova.org.za/novaimpactaccountingstandard/expert-threshold |
| Reputation weighting | The adjustment applied to a review score when a reviewer's Knowledge & Skills reputation is below the expert threshold; calculated as the ratio of the reviewer's reputation to the expert threshold. | https://nova.org.za/novaimpactaccountingstandard/reputation-weighting |
| Validation point | A scoring unit awarded when a reviewer provides a positive response to a validation review question for a specific reviewable aspect of an activity or impact. | https://nova.org.za/novaimpactaccountingstandard/validation-point |
| Verification point | A scoring unit awarded when a reviewer provides a positive response to a verification review question for a specific reviewable aspect of an activity or impact. | https://nova.org.za/novaimpactaccountingstandard/verification-point |
